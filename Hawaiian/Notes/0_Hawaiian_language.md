## Hawaiian language  

Hawaiian ('olelo Hawai'i) is a critically endangered Polynesian language of the Austronesian language family, originating in and native to the Hawaiian Islands. It is the historic native language of the Hawaiian people. Hawaiian, along with English, is an official language of the U.S. state of Hawai'i.  

**Language family**  
**Austronesian**  
* Malayo-Polynesian  
* Oceanic  
* Polynesian  
* Eastern Polynesian  
* Marquesic  
* Hawaiian  

Native speakers ~ 300   

Writing system - Latin (Hawaiian alphabet) and **HAwaiian Braille**

_In 1896, the Republic of Hawaii passed Act 57, an English-only law which subsequently banned Hawaiian language as the medium of instruction in publicly funded schools and promoted strict physical punishment of children caught speaking the Hawaiian language in schools._  

_Hawaiian language was not allowed to be used in public schools for 91 years. English essentially displaced Hawaiian on six of seven inhabited islands._
